PGO

Overview​

PGO is a patch programmable gain and offset processor. It is a core building block for low level patches, where the synthesist desires the ability to patch their own complex functional footprints from the ground up.

PGO is capable of a wide array of common processing functions: gain, offset, attenuation, sum, difference, inversion, offset, bipolar/unipolar conversions, single ended/differential conversions, and video line driving.

Integration​

Our advice on integrating this module into your system.

  • Consider it as a way to expand the functionality of modules you patch heavily. Add one next door to your favorite oscillators or RGB functions to expand modulation or signal input options.

  • Consider it as a low level analogue computing block. Several of these modules together can be patched to design your own processing functions or even replicate some of the functions from other modules. However, this level of flexibility comes at the expense of greater system size and more complex patches. Having a few lower level modules and a few higher level ones is a great strategy for getting the most out of your system.

  • Consider getting duplicates. Traditional analogue computers would have provided several instances of summing and difference amplifiers. For example, a bidirectional converter or scaler requires two modules. A triple colorspace function requires three modules. Some larger vector functions will require eight or more.

Installation​

Requirements​

  • EuroRack enclosure.
  • 12V DC or EuroRack power supply.
  • 2.1mm DC barrel power cable or a EuroRack power cable (both options included).
  • Two M2.5 x 6mm mounting screws, or whatever was provided by your enclosure's manufacturer.
  • #1 Phillips head screwdriver, or whatever was recommended by your enclosure's manufacturer.

Procedure​

Observe the following procedure when installing the module in your EuroRack enclosure.

  • Power off and disconnect your EuroRack enclosure's power supply and any attached DC adapters.
  • Attach the EuroRack power cable or the DC barrel power cable to the module. Do not connect both. Only connect one.
  • Carefully test fit the module with its attached power cable in the open space in your EuroRack enclosure. If it is obstructed by the enclosure or any internal assemblies, abort this procedure.
  • Connect the hanging end of the power cable to your power supply.
  • Mount the module to the EuroRack rails using all mounting holes.
  • Store your unused cable along with the product box in a safe location.
  • You may now power on your EuroRack enclosure and start patching.

Maintenance​

Keep your module free of dust and debris by performing periodic cleaning. Spots may be cleaned from the frontpanel with a microfiber cloth and isopropyl alcohol or other electronics cleaner.

Requirements​

Setup​

  • Configure your power supply to provide 12 volts to a 2.1mm DC barrel connector.
  • Set power supply over current protection to 100mA.
  • Connect power to the device.

Test +3.3V rail accuracy​
