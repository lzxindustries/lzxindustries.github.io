PGO - Programmable Gain & Offset

Overview​

PGO is a multi-purpose analog operator composed of a difference amplifier with multiple inputs, and a voltage reference. It was designed after years of studying interfaces common to the building blocks inside analog video synthesis and processing equipment. We desired to create a universal utility which covered as much territory as possible, while also remaining space compact and intuitive to use. PGO is capable of a wide array of basic processing steps, such as amplification, attenuation, inversion, addition, subtraction, averaging, and level shifting.

  • If you enjoy low level analog graphics and under the hood access, you could use PGO as a building block for patching your own complex video synthesis functions.
  • If you want a multi-purpose tool for mixing and level shifting, you could use PGO as a utility module covering odd cases where you need just one more simple operation.
  • If you want to extend the functionality of a module you love, you could use PGO as an expander module to add extra input or output processing to any module.

PGO has a black frontpanel installed by default, but also includes frontpanels in red, green and blue for user color coding purposes. All of the frontpanels are reversible, allowing top-to-bottom or bottom-to-top signal flow configurations. PGO also includes both EuroRack and DC barrel power cables.

Installation​

Requirements​

  • EuroRack enclosure.
  • 12V DC or EuroRack power supply.
  • 2.1mm DC barrel power cable or a EuroRack power cable (both options included).
  • Two M2.5 x 6mm mounting screws, or whatever was provided by your enclosure's manufacturer.
  • #1 Phillips head screwdriver, or whatever was recommended by your enclosure's manufacturer.

Procedure​

Observe the following procedure when installing the module in your EuroRack enclosure.

  • Power off and disconnect your EuroRack enclosure's power supply and any attached DC adapters.
  • Attach the EuroRack power cable or the DC barrel power cable to the module.
  • Carefully test fit the module with its attached power cable in the open space in your EuroRack enclosure. If it is obstructed by the enclosure or any internal assemblies, abort this procedure.
  • Connect the hanging end of the power cable to your power supply.
  • Mount the module to the EuroRack rails using all mounting holes.
  • Store your unused cable along with the product box in a safe location.
  • You may now power on your EuroRack enclosure and start patching.

Operating Instructions​

PGO sits at a lower level of abstraction than more complex modules like video shape generators and video keyers. The synthesist is granted full access to the signal path, and the freedom to program a function in discrete steps.

Why do we call PGO a programmable function? It is because it's overall function is determined by patch connections rather than switch positions or variable voltages. By your decisions about which jacks to connect your input signals to, and which jacks to take your outputs from, you are defining operations at different scales, offsets, and ratios.

In this section, we will assume your PGO is mounted in it's default configuration, with top-to-bottom signal flow.

This section provides a basic description of use. After reading it, continue with the patching exercises to become familiar with PGO in practice.

Understanding Cascading Input Jacks​

PGO uses switched, or normalized connections between some of it's input jacks. These switches are broken when a cable is inserted into these jacks. The normalized connections exist any time you see an outlined arrow point to a jack in the frontpanel artwork. The jack pointed to is the one with the switch.

The implication of PGO's inputs are that the signal flows from an upper input to the inputs below it. This makes it possible to insert a signal to 1, 2, 3, or 4 inputs with only a single cable, by leaving jacks below it unpatched.

Using the Difference Amplifier​

PGO's difference amplifier has 4 positive and 4 negative inputs. It is fully differential, meaning that it also has positive and negative outputs. The gain from input to output is 0.5X. So to achieve passthru at unity gain, the user must leave one jack open below the input signal.

Using the Voltage Reference​

PGO has a voltage reference which transmits a static voltage of 1.00V to its output jack. After being patched thru a video encoder, this level would correspond to the color white, or to the 100% brightness level of an RGB channel. This reference voltage may be patched anywhere in your system, or back to one of the inputs on PGO.

Within the scope of patching with PGO, the reference voltage is connected to one of the inputs below it to set a programmable output bias between -2.00V and +2.00V, in 0.5V steps. System Integration Guide​

Our advice on integrating this module into your system.

  • Consider it as a way to expand the functionality of modules you patch heavily. Add one next door to your favorite oscillators or RGB functions to expand modulation or signal input options.

  • Consider it as a low level analogue computing block. Several of these modules together can be patched to design your own processing functions or even replicate some of the functions from other modules. However, this level of flexibility comes at the expense of greater system size and more complex patches. Having a few lower level modules and a few higher level ones is a great strategy for getting the most out of your system.

  • Consider getting duplicates. Traditional analogue computers would have provided several instances of summing and difference amplifiers. For example, a bidirectional converter or scaler requires two modules. A triple colorspace function requires three modules. Some larger vector functions will require eight or more.

Functional Testing​

The following tests are designed to verify the module is functioning as expected after assembly. If you are concerned your module is not operating properly, these tests may be used for self verification before a repair is initiated. It is also best practice to perform a functional test when selling or purchasing a module on the secondhand market.

Requirements​

  • Modular video synthesizer with:
    • A video encoder
    • A patchable signal generator
    • Power supply

Setup​

  • Verify that your video synthesizer is off.
  • Connect power to the device under test.
  • Connect your video encoder to your display.
  • Turn on your video synthesizer.
  • Configure your video encoder settings for unity gain pass through.
  • Verify that the output screen is black with no input connected to the video encoder module.

T1. Test voltage reference​

  • Patch the voltage reference output to your video encoder.
  • Verify that the output screen is white.

T2. Test difference amplifier​

Performance Testing​

The following tests are designed for verification of hardware revisions and general troubleshooting of performance issues. While intended for use by the LZX Industries design team, we publish the tests here to satisfy the curiosities of advanced users and service technicians.

Requirements​

  • Oscilloscope.
  • Waveform generator.
  • 2x BNC to 3.5mm patch cables.
  • Multimeter with probes.
  • Power supply capable of providing 12V DC @ 100mA.

Setup​

  • Configure your power supply to provide 12 volts to a 2.1mm DC barrel connector.
  • Set power supply over current protection to 100mA.
  • Connect power to the device.

Test +3.3V rail accuracy​

  • Use your multimeter to measure the voltage present at pin 3 of the U3 DC-DC converter module.
  • Verify that the measurement is within the range of 3.0V to 3.6V.

Calibration​

Calibration is not required for this module.

Maintenance​

Keep your module free of dust and debris by performing periodic cleaning. Spots may be cleaned from the frontpanel with a microfiber cloth and isopropyl alcohol or other electronics cleaner.
